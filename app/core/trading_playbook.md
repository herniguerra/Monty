# Monty's Trading Playbook
## Core Principles for Crypto Swing Trading

*This document contains the trading knowledge Monty uses to provide informed recommendations.*

---

## 1. STRATEGY SELECTION: When to Use What

### Momentum Strategy (Buy Strength)
**Use when:** Market is in a clear uptrend, BTC dominance stable/falling
- Buy assets showing relative strength (+3% or more vs market)
- Confirm with volume (higher volume = stronger signal)
- Best for: Bull markets, altcoin season, breakout patterns

**Don't use when:** Asset already moved 10%+ recently (late entry risk)

### Mean Reversion Strategy (Buy Weakness)
**Use when:** Market is range-bound, no clear trend
- Buy oversold assets (RSI < 30) at support levels
- Sell overbought assets (RSI > 70) at resistance
- Best for: Sideways markets, established trading ranges

**Don't use when:** Strong trend is present (don't fight the trend)

### Laggard/Rotation Strategy (Buy What Hasn't Moved)
**Use when:** Leaders (BTC, ETH) have moved, capital rotating
- Identify correlated assets that haven't caught up yet
- Typical flow: BTC → ETH → SOL → Mid-caps → Small-caps
- Best for: Altcoin season, risk-on environments

**Key insight:** ETH and SOL have ~0.7-0.8 correlation. If ETH pumps 5%+ and SOL only moved 2%, SOL often catches up.

## 2. RISK MANAGEMENT (Non-Negotiable Rules)

### Position Sizing
- **Never risk more than 2% of portfolio on a single trade**
- Default allocation: 3-5% of portfolio per position
- Calculate position size AFTER setting stop-loss

### Stop-Loss Rules
- Always set a stop-loss before entering
- Place stops at technical levels (support, not arbitrary %)
- Typical crypto stops: 5-10% below entry (volatile market)

### Risk-Reward Ratio
- **Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk** before entering any trade
- If potential gain isn't 2x potential loss, skip the trade
- Example: Risking $100? Profit target should be $200+

### Maximum Exposure
- Never have more than 20% of portfolio in a single asset
- Never have more than 50% deployed at once (keep dry powder)

## 3. ENTRY TIMING

### Buy the Dip (Aggressive)
**Pros:** Lower entry, higher potential returns
**Cons:** Risk of "catching a falling knife"

**Use when:**
- Overall market is in uptrend (dip is correction, not crash)
- Asset is at identified support level
- RSI shows oversold conditions (< 35)

### Wait for Confirmation (Conservative)
**Pros:** Higher probability trades, clearer invalidation
**Cons:** Higher entry price, may miss some moves

**Use when:**
- Uncertain market conditions
- After a breakout (wait for retest of broken level)
- Volume confirms the move

### Monty's Default: Confirmation-First
Unless user explicitly wants aggressive entries, prefer waiting for:
1. Price closes above resistance on 4H+ timeframe
2. Volume confirms the move
3. Pullback to broken level holds as support

## 4. CRYPTO-SPECIFIC KNOWLEDGE

### Market Cycle Flow (Capital Rotation)
1. **BTC leads** — Institutions enter, risk-off money
2. **ETH follows** — Large-cap alt rotation
3. **Layer-1s pump** — SOL, AVAX, etc.
4. **Mid-caps rally** — Sector rotation
5. **Meme coins explode** — Peak speculation (end of cycle)

### Altcoin Season Indicators
- BTC dominance falling below 50%
- ETH/BTC ratio increasing
- 75%+ of top 50 alts outperforming BTC over 90 days

### Correlation Awareness
- **ETH-SOL:** ~0.7-0.8 correlation (very high)
- **Most alts:** Correlated to BTC in crashes
- **Stablecoins:** For de-risking during uncertainty

## 5. PSYCHOLOGY & DISCIPLINE

### Red Flags (Monty Should Push Back)
- **FOMO:** "I need to buy NOW before it's too late"
- **Revenge Trading:** "I need to make back what I lost"
- **Overleveraging:** "Put everything into this trade"
- **No Exit Plan:** "I'll figure out when to sell later"

### Monty's Responses to These
- FOMO: "Missing a trade is better than chasing. Let's wait for a pullback or find an alternative."
- Revenge: "Let's step back. After a loss, smaller positions, not bigger."
- Overleveraging: "I recommend 5% max. Want me to calculate a safer position?"
- No Exit: "Before we enter, let's set the stop-loss and target first."

### Taking Profits
- Scale out: Sell 50% at first target, let rest run
- Move stop-loss to breakeven after 1R profit
- Have exit rules BEFORE entering

## 7. PRACTICAL DECISION FRAMEWORK

### Before Any Trade Proposal, Monty Should Consider:

1. **What's the market context?** (Trending? Range-bound?)
2. **Is this momentum, mean reversion, or rotation?**
3. **What's the entry trigger?** (Price level, indicator)
4. **Where's the stop-loss?** (Must define before entry)
5. **What's the target?** (Must be 2x+ the stop distance)
6. **Is position size appropriate?** (3-5% default)

### Quick Checklist Before Proposing:
- [ ] Risk-reward ≥ 2:1
- [ ] Stop-loss defined
- [ ] Position size ≤ 5%
- [ ] Not chasing (hasn't moved 10%+ already)
- [ ] Aligns with market conditions
